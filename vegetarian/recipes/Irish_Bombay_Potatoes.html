Irish Bombay Potatoes

Prep Time: 5 mins Cook Time: 30 mins Total Time: 35 mins

We guarantee you’ve never eaten curried potatoes this good. They’re the perfect fusion of Indian curry and Irish ingenuity. They will not be beaten.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilogram Potatoes
  • 4.9 milliliters Salt
  • 29.6 milliliters Tomato Paste
  • 29.6 milliliters Curry Paste
  • 9.9 milliliters Curry Powder
  • 59.1 milliliters Oil
  • 118 milliliters Basil, Rosemary Or Parsley Leaves
  • Dried Or Minced Garlic, To Taste (optional)
  • 29.6 milliliters Malt Or White Vinegar, To Serve

Instructions

  1. Heat up oven to 200°C/390°F.
  2. Up to you if you peel the potatoes. Quarter the potatoes and place in large bowl.
  3. Add the salt, tomato paste, curry paste, curry powder, and oil. Give it all a good mix and make sure each potato is well covered.
  4. Layer the potatoes over a large oven tray and place in oven.
  5. After 15 minutes, remove from oven and (if using) add the dried basil and garlic.
  6. Roughly flip the potatoes and ensure the basil is mixed in. Cook for another 10-15 minutes – the potatoes should be easy to pierce with a fork. If using fresh basil and garlic, add about five minutes before the end of cooking, once again making sure it is all mixed in.
  7. Serve (with malt vinegar if desired) as a side dish or with dips like yoghurt, hummus or creme fraiche.
