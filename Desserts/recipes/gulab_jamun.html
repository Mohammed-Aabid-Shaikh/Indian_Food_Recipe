Gulab Jamun

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 30 mins Total Time: 45 mins

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made with khoya (milk solids) and soaked in rose-flavored sugar syrup. These soft, spongy, and syrupy balls are a must-have for festive celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1-2 tablespoons milk (as needed)
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add milk gradually and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls, ensuring they are smooth and crack-free.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over low-medium heat. Fry the dough balls until golden brown, ensuring they cook evenly.
  4. In another pan, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until slightly thickened. Add cardamom powder, rose water, and saffron strands.
  5. Soak the fried dough balls in warm sugar syrup for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Serve the Gulab Jamuns warm or chilled, garnished with chopped pistachios if desired.
