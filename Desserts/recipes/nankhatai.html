Nankhatai

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 20 mins Total Time: 35 mins

Nankhatai is a traditional Indian shortbread cookie that is crumbly, buttery, and melts in your mouth. It's a perfect tea-time snack and a festive favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (rava)
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, sift together all-purpose flour, gram flour, semolina, and baking soda.
  3. Add powdered sugar and cardamom powder to the dry ingredients and mix well.
  4. Gradually add ghee and mix to form a smooth dough. Do not over-knead.
  5. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into balls. Flatten slightly.
  6. Place the dough balls on the baking tray and garnish with chopped pistachios and almonds.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the edges turn light golden brown.
  8. Remove from the oven and let them cool completely before serving or storing.
