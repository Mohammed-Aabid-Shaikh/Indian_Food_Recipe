Rasgulla

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 30 mins Total Time: 45 mins

Rasgulla is a popular Bengali sweet made from fresh paneer or chhena. These spongy, syrup-soaked balls are a beloved dessert in India, especially during festivals.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan. Once it comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add lemon juice, stirring until the milk curdles.
  2. Strain the curdled milk to separate the whey from the chhena (paneer). Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the lemon flavor.
  3. Knead the chhena until smooth and form small, smooth balls.
  4. In a separate pan, combine water and sugar and bring it to a boil. Add the chhena balls to the syrup and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until they double in size.
  5. Remove from the syrup, add cardamom powder and rose water (if using), and let the Rasgullas cool in the syrup.
  6. Serve chilled and enjoy!
