Rasmalai

Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 40 mins Total Time: 1 hour

Rasmalai is a royal Indian dessert made of soft, spongy paneer discs soaked in creamy, saffron-flavored milk. Perfect for festive occasions, it melts in your mouth with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for paneer)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for rabri)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for rabri)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons chopped pistachios and almonds
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 liter of milk in a pan. Once it boils, add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle the milk. Strain and wash with cold water to remove the acidic taste. Hang the paneer in a muslin cloth for 30 minutes to remove excess water.
  2. Knead the paneer until smooth and divide into small, flat discs.
  3. In a deep pan, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling 1 cup sugar with 4 cups water. Add the paneer discs and cook for 15 minutes. They will puff up. Remove and cool.
  4. In another pan, boil 1 liter of milk for the rabri. Simmer until reduced to half. Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron, and chopped nuts. Mix well.
  5. Soak the cooked paneer discs in the prepared rabri and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  6. Serve chilled, garnished with additional nuts and a few saffron strands.
