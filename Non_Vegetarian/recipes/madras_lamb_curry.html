Madras Lamb Curry

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 1 hour 15 mins Total Time: 1 hour 45 mins

Madras Lamb Curry is a fiery South Indian dish, bursting with bold flavors from aromatic spices and tender lamb cooked to perfection. Enjoy it with steamed rice or buttery naan!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams lamb (cut into cubes)
  • 2 large onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, green cardamom)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh curry leaves (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add whole spices and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add lamb pieces and cook until browned.
  5. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Pour in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  7. Add coconut milk and bring to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 1 hour or until the lamb is tender.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh curry leaves before serving.
  10. Serve hot with naan, paratha, or steamed rice.
