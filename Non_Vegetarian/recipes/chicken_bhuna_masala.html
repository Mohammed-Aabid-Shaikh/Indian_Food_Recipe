Chicken Bhuna Masala

Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 40 mins Total Time: 1 hour

Chicken Bhuna Masala is a classic Indian dish known for its rich, thick gravy made by slow-cooking chicken with aromatic spices. Best paired with naan or jeera rice!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams chicken (bone-in or boneless)
  • 2 large onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, green cardamom)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add whole spices and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  5. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Pour in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  7. Reduce the heat, add yogurt, and cook while stirring continuously for 5 minutes.
  8. Cover and simmer on low heat until the chicken is cooked and the gravy thickens.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
  10. Serve hot with naan, paratha, or steamed rice.
