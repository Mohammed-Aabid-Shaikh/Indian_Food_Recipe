Vada Pav

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 20 mins Total Time: 35 mins

Vada Pav, often called the "Indian burger," is a beloved street food from Mumbai. It features a spiced potato fritter (vada) sandwiched in a soft bun (pav) with chutneys and fried green chilies.

Ingredients

  • 4 pav buns
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (to make batter)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
  • Chutneys (green chutney and tamarind chutney)
  • Fried green chilies for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add green chilies, turmeric powder, and mashed potatoes. Mix well.
  2. Add red chili powder, coriander leaves, and salt. Mix thoroughly and let the mixture cool.
  3. Shape the potato mixture into small balls (vada).
  4. In a bowl, mix gram flour, baking soda, salt, and water to form a thick batter.
  5. Dip each potato ball into the batter and deep-fry until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  6. Slice the pav buns and spread green chutney on one side and tamarind chutney on the other.
  7. Place a vada inside the bun and garnish with fried green chilies. Serve hot.
