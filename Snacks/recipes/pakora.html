Pakora

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 20 mins Total Time: 35 mins

Pakoras are a popular Indian snack made by deep-frying spiced gram flour-coated vegetables or other ingredients. Crispy, flavorful, and perfect for tea-time!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 small potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 small bowl of spinach leaves (optional)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, carom seeds, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Gradually add water to form a thick, smooth batter.
  3. Add sliced onions, potatoes, and spinach leaves (if using) to the batter. Mix well to coat the vegetables evenly.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of the batter-coated vegetables into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove pakoras with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve hot with green chutney or ketchup.
